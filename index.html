Infection Control Recap

Key Principles for Dental Assistants

1. Infection Risks & Hazards

  • Sharps Injuries: From needles, scalers, burs, and other instruments.
  • Aerosols & Splatter: Generated by high-speed handpieces, ultrasonic scalers, and air-water syringes.
  • Contaminated Surfaces: Contact with countertops, light handles, dental chairs, and equipment.
  • Direct Contact: Exposure to blood, saliva, and other potentially infectious materials (OPIM).

2. Control Measures

  • Hand Hygiene: The single most important measure.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Correct use of gloves, masks, eyewear, and gowns.
  • Instrument Sterilisation: Following cleaning, disinfecting, and sterilisation protocols.
  • Surface Asepsis: Using barriers and disinfectants on clinical contact surfaces.
  • Waste Management: Correctly disposing of sharps and contaminated waste.

3. Transmission-Based Precautions

Used in addition to Standard Precautions for patients with known or suspected infections.

  • Contact Precautions: Requires gloves and a gown.
  • Droplet Precautions: Requires a surgical mask.
  • Airborne Precautions: Requires a P2/N95 respirator.
  • Enhanced Cleaning: Increased frequency and thoroughness of cleaning.

4. Breaches in Infection Control

A breach is any failure in protocol. Your response must be immediate.

  • Identify the Breach: Recognise events like a sharps injury, steriliser failure, or a splash.
  • Respond Safely: Perform first aid and remove yourself from the hazardous situation.
  • Communicate Clearly: Report the incident to your supervisor immediately.

5. Risk Assessment & Immediate Action

Following a breach, you must assess the level of risk to determine the next steps.

  • Assess the Risk: Consider patient history, fluid type, and exposure severity.
  • Take Immediate Action: Follow your workplace's specific post-exposure protocol.
  • Document Everything: Fill out an incident report form accurately and promptly.
  • Seek Medical Advice: Your employer should guide you on seeking medical evaluation.
